Spring 2022

The seminar was held in 341 Altgeld Hall at 3pm each Friday. The speakers and titles can be found below. In Fall 2022, this seminar was organized by Zach Halladay. As of Fall 2023, information about this seminar can be found at https://gshts.github.io/.

  • Organizational Meeting (January 28th)
  • Brian Shin (February 11th): An Introduction to Exodromy.
  • Timmy Feng (February 18th): Formalism of six operations and derived algebraic stacks (notes).
  • Zach Halladay (February 25th): Introduction to Equivariant Homotopy Theory and RO(G)-graded Cohomology.
  • No speaker (March 4th)
  • Likun Xie (March 11th): Applications of Dualizing Complex in Commutative Algebra.
    • Spring Break
  • Yigal Kamel (March 25th): An introduction to 2-categories (notes).
  • Liz Tatum (April 1st): Splitting $BP<2> ⋀ BP<2>$ at primes $p \ge 5$.
  • Samuel Hsu (April 8th): Towards a universal property of the ∞-equipment of enriched (∞,1)-categories.
  • Doron Grossman-Naples (April 15th): You Already Care About ∞-Topoi.
  • Langwen Hui (April 22nd): Rational Homotopy Theory.
  • No speaker (April 29th)
  • Johnson Tan (May 6th): Calculus for Algebraic Topologists.

Fall 2021

The seminar was held in 243 Altgeld Hall at 3pm each Friday. The speakers and titles can be found below.

  • Heyi Zhu (August 30th): A p-complete Hopkins-Mahowald Theorem.
  • Likun Xie (October 1st): On the Lichtenbaum-Quillen conjectures in algebraic K theory.
  • Doron Grossman-Naples (October 8th): Simplicial Localizations and How to Find Them.
  • Samuel Hsu (October 15th): Cellular Homotopy Type Theory: why and how?
  • Timmy Feng (October 22nd): An introduction to equivariant motivic homotopy theory.
  • Johnson Tan (October 29th): Connections between motivic and classical homotopy theory.
  • No speaker (November 5th)
  • Abhra Kundu (November 12th): ∞-categorical Kummer theory.
  • Connor Grady (November 19th): Introduction to the Stolz-Teichner Program.
    • Break for Thanksgiving
  • Liz Tatum (December 3rd): Towards Splitting BP<2>⋀BP<2> at Odd Primes.
